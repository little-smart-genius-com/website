# Unlock Neurodiverse Potential with Word Scrambles

*By Little Smart Genius · Little Smart Genius*

---

Ever feel like traditional worksheets just don’t click for your child? You’re not alone. Many activities can feel rigid and frustrating, especially for neurodiverse learners. That’s why we designed our word scramble activity with a different goal in mind. It’s not just about spelling a list of words correctly. The real magic is in building **cognitive flexibility**—the brain’s ability to switch gears and see things from new angles. Think of it as fun mental gymnastics that strengthens crucial learning muscles.

Setting up for success is simple and takes just minutes, but a few thoughtful preparations make all the difference. **Always print two copies of the worksheet.** Use one as intended, and cut the other up to create movable letter tiles. This simple adaptation removes the pressure of writing and lets kids focus on the puzzle of letter arrangement, which can be a game-changer for those who struggle with fine motor skills or get overwhelmed by a blank page.

The activity itself is full of built-in adaptations. You can start by revealing the first letter of a word, or work together side-by-side. The key is to follow your child’s pace and celebrate the process, not just the perfect answer. For younger children or those needing more support, you might focus on just one or two words. For older kids ready for a challenge, try timing the activity or turning it into a friendly race.

To keep the engagement going, the full article shares creative variations like using the words in a silly story or hunting for the completed words in a favorite book. These extensions help solidify learning in a joyful, low-pressure way.
