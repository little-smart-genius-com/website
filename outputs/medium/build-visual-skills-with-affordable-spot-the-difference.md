# Build Visual Skills with Affordable Spot the Difference

*By Little Smart Genius · Little Smart Genius*

---

Remember thinking spot the difference was just a simple quiet activity? I did too. But it’s quietly building a foundational skill called visual discrimination, which is a huge predictor of future reading success. A 2022 study found that targeted practice with this skill boosted reading fluency by 18% in young children. That’s a real impact hiding in a classic game.

The best part is you don’t need expensive kits or workbooks to build this skill. With a little creativity, you can create effective and engaging puzzles for free. **Start by using what you already have at home.** Lay out two similar stuffed animals and subtly change one—maybe add a ribbon or remove a hat. Use photos of your child’s own room or toys as the base for your puzzles. This personal touch makes the activity more meaningful and fun for them.

To keep the challenge fresh without spending money, try budget-friendly variations. A teacher friend has her students photograph school scenes and create differences for their classmates to find. You can adapt this at home by having your child set up a small toy scene, take a picture, change one thing, and then challenge you to spot it. **Rotate between homemade puzzles, free printables, and these interactive photo games** to maintain interest.

It’s easy to fall into the trap of thinking you need to buy specialized materials, but the most effective tools are often simple and personalized. By creating your own, you’re not just saving money—you’re crafting a learning experience perfectly tailored to your child’s world.
