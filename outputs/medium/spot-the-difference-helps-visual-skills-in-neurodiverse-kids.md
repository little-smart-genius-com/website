# Spot the Difference Helps Visual Skills in Neurodiverse Kids

*By Little Smart Genius · Little Smart Genius*

---

Have you ever watched your child stare at a puzzle, their brain working so hard you can almost see the gears turning? That focus is building something crucial: their visual processing skills. For neurodiverse kids with ADHD, dyslexia, or autism, strengthening this "brain's first filter" is especially powerful. It’s the foundation for everything from reading to social cues, and targeted activities can make a real difference.

The magic lies in choosing the right tool for your child's unique brain. A simple spot-the-difference puzzle isn't just a game; it's a flexible tool you can adapt. For a younger child or one on the autism spectrum, photorealistic images of familiar scenes—like a real city street—offer the predictability their brains crave. For an older child with ADHD, the same puzzle becomes a manageable challenge to practice sustained attention. The activity stays fun, but the developmental goal shifts beautifully with their needs.

Setting up for success is half the battle. You can transform a potentially frustrating task into an empowering one by tweaking the environment. Think about creating a dedicated "focus station" with minimal clutter. Some kids thrive at a standing desk where they can move quietly, while others need noise-canceling headphones to filter out distraction. The goal is to reduce external stressors so their brain can fully engage with the visual challenge in front of them.
